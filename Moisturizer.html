moisturizer

Now that your skin is nice and clean, we need to moisturize! When you cleanse, the oils that help keep our skin supple and moisturised are removed no matter how gentle your cleanser is. We use a moisturizer to restore the moisture and add other beneficial ingredients to our skin. A good moisturizer nourishes the skin and improves our skin's barrier. That helps treat and prevent obvious things like dryness, but also less-obvious concerns like oiliness, acne, fine lines, and leftover acne marks. It’s especially important to have a good moisturizer (or a few!) if you plan on using any potentially irritating actives in the future.

There are many types of moisturizing products - from the very light to the very thick. Typically, the thicker the product, the more moisturizing it is. You may find one moisturizer that you love, or you may want to layer a couple different products. Play around with it and see what works for you! It's important that moisturizer leaves your skin feeling bouncy and supple - happy and healthy, not dry and tight.

How To Use

You should always apply moisturizer on clean skin. For best results, apply a nickle sized amount of moisturizer shortly after cleansing, before your skin is totally dry. Moisturizers are most effective if you use them while your skin is still damp because damp skin absorbs the product more readily. That also gives the moisturizer the chance to lock in that hydration.

If you are layering products, you want to start with the lightest products and work your way up to the thickest product. The idea is that, by layering products in this order, the thicker ones won’t prevent the thinner ones from getting to your skin.

Remember to be gentle with your skin try to apply in upwards motions to prevent premature wrinkles and even saggy skin over time. If a product causes serious irritation (eg.stinging, rashes, prolonged redness) drop it!

Types of Moisturizers

Moisturizers typically contain 3 kinds of ingredients: humectants, emollients, and/or occlusives. See the table below to see a break down of each ingredient type.

Ingredient type Description Ingredients to look for

Humectant
  • Draws water to skin cells to keep skin hydrated
  • Binds water to the skin to help keep it on the surface
  • Absorbs very quickly
  • Lightweight consistency

Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, Honey, Aloe Vera

Emollients
  • Space fillers
  • The lipids and oils fill in all the cracks on the skin
  • Helps smooth out the surface of your skin and repair your skin’s barrier
  • Creamy consistency

Silicone (Dimethicone, Cyclomethicone), Lanolin, Shea Butter, Squalane, Grape Seed Oils, Ceramides, Rosehip

Occlusives
  • Seals in moisture
  • Occlusives are thicker in consistency
  • Blocks the evaporation of water

Mineral Oil, Lanolin, Beeswax, Olive Oil, Shea Butter

Gels

These usually contain humectants and have a cooling effect on the skin. They absorb pretty easily so they are suitable for a all different skin types. Gels are especially great for those who want lightweight hydration without the greasy feeling.

Some popular gel moisturizers are: Neutrogena Hydro Boost Hyaluronic Acid Hydrating Water Gel, Sebamed Clear Face Care Gel, and Hada Labo Skin Plumping Gel Cream.

Creams

Creams are a thick consistency and contain mostly occlusive ingrdeients. They are especially beneficial you have dry or irritated skin. This will ensure that your skin is properly hydrated without being clogged up.

Some popular creams are: First Aid Beauty Ultra Repair Cream, Cetaphil Moisturizing Cream, and Aveeno Eczema Therapy Daily Moisturizing Cream.

Balms

Balms are the thickest moisturizers available on the market and they have a high concentration of oils and lipids compared to water. This ratio makes them a bad fit for oily and combination skin types. They have a sticky feeling and they sit heavy on the skin and many may be comedogenic so they are onyl suitable for very dry skin types.

Some popular balms are: Aquaphor Healing Ointment and Cerave Healing Ointment.

Lotions

For those who suffer from oily or combination skin, lotions can be extremely helpful. These are lightweight, yet offer a sufficient amount of hydration. Their main ingredient type is emolient so make sure you choose one that’s non-comedogenic (that does not clog pores).

Some popular lotions are: Kind to Skin Replenishing Rich Moisturizer , CeraVe Facial Moisturizing Lotion, and Bioderma Sensibio Light Cream.
